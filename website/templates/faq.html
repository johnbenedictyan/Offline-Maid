Employer Eligibilty

Can an Employment Pass or S Pass holder employ a FDW?

Yes. The applicant needs to provide the following:

  • Passport
  • Work pass or spouse's Long-Term Pass
  • Child's birth certificate or student pass(for Long Term Visit Pass holders)
  • Income Tax Notice of Assessment for the current or previous assessment year

What is the minimum income needed to employ a FDW?

There is no specified amount. It is assessed based on the type of application:

  • For married couples, you can combine your income with your spouse's
  • For the Joint Income Scheme, you can combine the income of family members who are living together
  • For people aged 60 years or older, you can apply under the Sponsorship Scheme

Can I employ a FDW if I do not have any income?

If you are 60 years old or older, not earning an income and not staying with any working adults, you can apply under the Sponsorship Scheme.

Can a single person employ a FDW?

The applicant will be assessed based on eligibility such as:

  • You must be 21 years old and above.
  • You cannot be an undischarged bankrupt.
  • You must be earning a steady source of income.

What are the requirements to hire a second FDW?

The applicant needs to have any of these family members living with him/her:

  • At least 2 children below 18 years old
  • A parent or parent-in-law over 60 years old

What is the Employers’ Orientation Programme?

It is a 3-hour programme for employers to understand their role and responsibilities.

First time employers and existing employers who have employed more than 3 different FDW within a 12-month period must complete the EOP before they can apply for a FDW.

Waiting Time & Cost

What is the waiting time for a FDW to arrive from overseas?

It is between 2 weeks to 8 weeks, depending on factors such as the nationality of the FDW, passport status, etc.

What is a Transfer Maid?

Transfer maid is a FDW who is already in Singapore under the employment of another employer and is seeking for a new employer.

What are the costs involved in employing a FDW?

The costs are as shown:

  • One-time placement fee charged by the agency for sourcing and bringing in the FDW.
  • FDW’s IPA, security bond, medical, SIP, insurance, work permit application and covid-19 related cost.
  • FDW’s monthly salary, levy and half yearly medical checkup.
  • FDW's repatriation cost.

What is a security bond?

Employers are required to place a S$5,000 security bond in the form of a banker's guarantee for the FDW he/she wants to employ.

The bond is required by the Government of Singapore.

It is used to ensure that both the employer and FDW comply with the conditions of the Work Permit.

What is SIP?

The Settling-In-Programme (SIP) is a 1-day orientation programme for first-time FDWs to educate them on safety precautions and living in Singapore.

What is FDW levy and how much is it?

The FDW levy is a pricing mechanism by the Ministry of Manpower (MOM) to regulate the number of foreign workers in Singapore.

Employers of FDW must pay a monthly levy of S$265 or concessionary rate of S$60.

Who qualifies for a levy concession?

Levy concession are available to:

  • Employers living with a citizen child, age 16 years old or below.
  • Employers is a citizen or permanent resident, age 65 years and above.
  • Employers living with a person who requires permanent assistance with ADL.

Are there any grant given to Singapore Citizen or PRs?

Employers with a family member who requires permanent assistance with 3 or more ADLs, may qualify for a monthly grant of S$120.

More information is available on the AIC’s Singapore Silver Pages website.

During Employment

What is the number of rest days for FDW?

The FDW is entitled to a weekly rest day as per MOM guideline.

If FDW agrees to work on her rest day, the employer must compensate her with 1 day’s salary per off day.*

*To avoid dispute, this agreement should be in writing.

Can employers help to safekeep FDW’s money?

From 1 January 2019, employers are not allowed to keep FDW’s salary, money, bank card and bank book belonging to her, even if she requests it.

Must employers pay for FDW's salary during training?

Employers must pay for FDW’s salary as long as she is employed by the employer, even when she is sent back to the agency for training.

Who is responsible for FDW's medical expenses?

The employer is liable to pay the full cost of medical expenses, including hospitalization.

The employer is required by MOM to buy medical insurance for the FDW, with a minimum cover of S$15,000 for inpatient care and day surgery.

What if the FDW goes missing?

The following steps should be taken:

  • The employer should try to look for the FDW and inform the employment agency.
  • The employer must file a police report if he/she fails to locate the FDW within 24 hours.
  • The employer will have one month to find and repatriate the FDW*.
  • *Failing to do so will result in the S$5,000 security bond being forfeited.

What if the FDW is pregnant?

Under MOM’s regulations, FDWs cannot be pregnant or give birth in Singapore.

Employers should repatriate the FDW back to her country of origin as soon as possible*

* Falling to do so will result in the S$5,000 security bond being forfeited.

What if the FDW falls sick or injured herself on her rest day?

Employers must pay for the costs of providing the necessary medical treatment even if the FDW’s illness or injury occurs on her rest day.

Will the FDW be paid when on overseas leave?

No, the employer need not do so unless this is provided for in the employment contract.

What if the FDW’s medical expenses exceed the insurance benefit limit?

Employers are not allowed to make the FDW pay for her medical expenses. This includes medical expenses that are above what the insurance pays.

Employers are encouraged to buy insurance with a higher coverage to protect themselves from any excessive medical bills.

Can employers reinstate the FDW’s Work Permit after cancellation?

Employers can submit requests to MOM and have to ensure that the Work Permit has not expired and the FDW is still in Singapore.

If approved, employers need to pay a $35 transaction fee. The FDW levy will be charged from the date of cancellation.

Do I have to pay for the FDW’s passport renewal?

Employers and FDW should mutually agree on who should pay for the passport renewal.

This should be decided preferably at the start of her employment and stated in the employment contract.

Can I extend the current FDW’s work permit until the new FDW arrives?

Yes. Employers have to submit an application 30 days before the work permit expires*.

*The extension will be up to one month.

Repatriation

What is the process for sending a FDW home?

When the FDW’s contract ends, employers need to buy a direct air ticket including check-in luggage to the international airport of the FDW’s hometown or nearest to her hometown.

If transit is required, the employer should give sufficient allowance for FDW to sustain herself during her transit.

Who pays for the air ticket if the FDW is sent home before her contract ends?

Employers are responsible for the cost of sending their FDW home even if the contract has not ended.

What if the FDW missed her flight home and her Work Permit has been cancelled?

Employers should book the FDW on another flight home and log in to WPOL to extend her special pass and change her departure date.
