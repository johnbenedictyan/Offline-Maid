C. During Employment (14)

Employers are liable to pay the full cost of medical expenses, including hospitalization.

Employers are required by MOM to buy medical insurance for the Maid, with a minimum cover of S$15,000 for inpatient care and day surgery.

Employers are encouraged to buy higher coverage insurance to protect themselves from any excessive medical bills

Employers and Maid should mutually agree on who should pay for the passport renewal. It should be in writing to avoid future disputes.

Maids are entitled to a weekly rest day as per MOM guideline.

If a Maid agrees to work on her rest day, the employer must compensate her with 1 day’s salary per off day.

The number of rest day per month agreed between a maid and his/her employer should be in written to avoid future dispute

Employers must pay for the costs of providing the necessary medical treatment even if the Maid’s illness or injury occurs on her rest day.

  • Employers should try to look for the Maid and inform the employment agency.
  • Employers must file a police report if he/she fails to locate the Maid within 24 hours.
  • Employers will have one month to find and repatriate the Maid. Failing to do so will result in the S$5,000 security bond being forfeited.

Under Ministry of Manpower regulations, maids cannot be pregnant or give birth in Singapore.

Employers should repatriate such maids back to their country of origin as soon as possible. Falling to do so will result in S$5,000 security bond being forfeited.

Employers do not need to pay maid salary when she is on overseas leave.

Employers can apply for a levy waiver for the duration the maid is away and defer her 6 monthly medical check.

To qualify for the levy waiver, the overseas leave must be at least 7 days and not more than 60 days.

The maid must show their work permit card when they re-enter Singapore.

From 1 January 2019, employers are not allowed to keep Maid’s salary, money, bank card and bank book belonging to them, even if they requests it.

Maids are only allowed to perform domestic chores at the residential address declare to Ministry of Manpower. However, if maids are required to look after elder or child at a relative’s house employer must:

  • Notify Ministry of Manpower before proceed with the arrangement.
  • Obtain maid’s written agreement to take care of child or elder at employer’s relative house.
  • Ensure maid does not perform the full load of housework in both households.
  • Ensure accommodation meets the requirements of Ministry of Manpower if maid needs to stay overnight to take care of child or elder.

Employers must pay maid salary even while overseas, example on holiday or business trip.

Employers need to seek Ministry of Manpower approval to leave maid with a friend, relative or employment agency while overseas.

Employers can submit request to Ministry of Manpower and has to ensure that Work Permit has not expired and maid is still in Singapore.
