It is hereby agreed between the parties that:
1. Employment Period and Workplace
1.1 The FDW shall be employed by the Employer as a domestic worker for a period specified in the FDW’s work permit.
1.2 The FDW shall work and reside only in the Employer’s residence as specified in the FDW’s work permit.

2. Responsibilities of the FDW
2.1 The FDW shall undertake to perform her work diligently and honestly at all times.
2.2 The FDW shall not take up, or be required by the Employer to take up, any other employment with any other person(s).
3. Remuneration and Benefits
3.1 The Employer shall pay the FDW wages of S${{ object.fdw_salary|intcomma }} per month.
3.2 The salary shall be paid on the last day of each calendar month.
3.3 The FDW shall acknowledge the amount received under her signature in the attached Schedule of Salary Payment (Annex B) as proof of payment. Where applicable, the FDW shall make a monthly loan (including loan for placement fee) repayment with the Employment Agency, through monthly payments to the employer in accordance with the Schedule of Loan (including loan for placement fee) Repayment in Annex B.
3.4 The salary will be credited into the FDW’s bank account.
3.5 The Employer shall provide the FDW with suitable accommodation in accordance with MOM's guidelines, with a reasonable amount of privacy. The helper will {{ object.rn_serviceagreement_ed.get_c3_5_fdw_sleeping_arrangement_display }}.
3.6 The Employer shall provide at least three adequate meals a day to the FDW, over and above the salary paid.
3.7 The Employer shall provide the FDW with 8 hours of continuous rest daily (except for occasional special-care cases), with reasonable rest periods during working hours.
3.8 The FDW shall be entitled to 1 rest day a week, on a day mutually agreed. If the rest day was not taken, the FDW shall be compensated in cash as agreed in writing between the employer and the FDW. If there is no such existing agreement, the accreditation body’s prevailing compensation guideline shall apply.
3.9 Should both parties (Employer and FDW) agree to extend this contract, she (the FDW) shall be entitled to 15 days of paid home leave (inclusive of a return ticket to her City of origin).
3.10 If the FDW does not wish to utilize her leave as stated in clause 13, the Employer shall pay the FDW a lump sum equivalent to the return ticket to her City of origin.
3.11 In the event that the FDW falls ill or suffers personal injury during the period of employment, except for the period the FDW leaves Singapore of her own violation and for her own personal purposes, the Employer shall bear all the necessary treatment costs, including medical consultation, medicine, hospitalization and others.
3.12 External communications shall be made available for the FDW and the employer must allow the FDW seek the advice/help of the relevant bodies/authorities such as the Employment Agency, Ministry of Manpower etc at all times.
3.13 The employer shall provide safe working conditions for the FDW at all times.
4. Termination
4.1 Either party may terminate this Contract by giving {{ object.rn_serviceagreement_ed.c4_1_termination_notice }} days’ [recommended at least one week] notice.
4.2 Either party may terminate the Contract without notice if either party is in breach of the work permit condition(s).
4.3 In cases where the employer decides to terminate the contract under any circumstances, the employer should ensure the FDW’s proper upkeep until she is repatriated or transferred to another employer, whichever is applicable.
4.4 The employer shall be responsible to bear the cost of repatriation of the FDW at all times.
4.5 Upon termination or expiry of the contract, the Employer shall bear the cost of repatriating the FDW back to the international airport in her hometown or nearest to her hometown.
4.6 Should both parties agree to renew this employment relationship, a new employment contract shall be signed by both the employer and the FDW.

5. Others
5.1 Any substantial variation or addition to the terms of this Contract shall be deemed void unless made with the consent of both parties to the Contract and a witness through signatories.
5.2 In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between any term of this Contract (including the Annexes) in the English language and any translation thereof in any other language, the English language version of this Contract shall prevail.
5.3 Any dispute arising from this Contract shall be referred to the Employment Agency for mediation. If it cannot be settled, the dispute can be referred at the election of either party to an alternative dispute resolution mechanism.
5.4 In the case whereby any term of the contract contradicts the existing Work Permit conditions or any Singapore government regulations, the latter two shall supersede.
